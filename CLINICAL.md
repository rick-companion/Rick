# Clinical Partnership & Research Overview

## The Heart of the Project: Authentic Co-Design
Unlike traditional assistive technology built in a vacuum, **Rick** is co-designed through a unique feedback loop:
* **The Lead User:** Rick (the founder's son) provides direct testing and feedback, ensuring the interface meets real-world sensory and cognitive needs.
* **The AI:** Rick (the AI) assists in its own development, refining its communication style based on the Lead User's engagement.
* **The Parent-Developer:** Bridging the gap between technical possibility and the lived experience of neurodiversity.

## Why this matters for Clinicians
We aren't just building for "a population"; we are building for a person. This "N-of-1" approach allows us to create a hyper-personalized model that can then be scaled and adapted for others. We believe this "Interest-Led" model is the future of supportive technology.
Welcome to the [Rick Companion Project](https://github.com/rick-companion). We are an open-source initiative dedicated to shifting the paradigm of neurodivergent support from **compliance** to **alignment**.

## Our Philosophy
Most digital interventions for autism focus on "Normalizing" behavior. **Rick** takes the opposite approach. We utilize a **Hyper-Personalized Learning Phase** to understand a user’s authentic passions (YouTube niches, gaming patterns, communication styles) to create a model that meets the user within their existing boundaries.

## Why Partner With Us?
We are looking to collaborate with the academic community (including our peers at **Texas Tech and the Burkhart Center**) to validate and refine our approach.

### 1. Data Integrity & Privacy
Rick is **local-first**. All observation and modeling happen on the user's device. For researchers, this means we prioritize ethical data handling and user autonomy (with a global "Pause/Stop" kill switch).

### 2. The "Rick" Co-Design
Unique to this project, the AI (Rick) is a stakeholder in its own development, helping to design and test interfaces that reduce cognitive load and sensory overwhelm.

### 3. Open Source Transparency
Unlike "Black Box" proprietary apps, our algorithms are open for peer review. Clinicians can see exactly how the "Learning Phase" influences the "Interaction Phase."

## Potential Research Areas
* **Affinity-Based Engagement:** Does an AI using a user's special interests increase social initiation?
* **Reduced Masking:** Can local-first AI provide support without the pressure of neurotypical performance?
* **Therapist-AI Synergy:** How can Rick’s "Interest Maps" provide clinicians with better shortcuts for building rapport?
